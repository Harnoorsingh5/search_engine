History of Cognos BI Features of IBM Cognos Component in Cognos How to Download and Install Cognos Types of Cognos Other major Important BI vendors Advantages of using Cognos Disadvantages of Cognos History of Cognos BI Cognos software was founded in 1969 by Alan Rushforth and Peter Glenister. It began as a consulting firm for the Canadian Government and offered its first software product, QUIZ, in 1979 Originally Quasar Systems Limited, it adopted the name Cognos in the year 1982. Cognos name is derived from a fragment scissor off the Latin word "cognosco. It means "knowledge from personal experience." In 1995, Ron Zambonini was appointed as a CEO of and Cognos. IBM formally announced its acquisition of Cognos in November 2007 for the sum of $4.9 billion. From January 1, 2009, the software is called "Cognos Bl and Financial Performance Management" or Cognos Bl and FPM. In January 2010, Cognos acquired SPSS were brought together to create the Business Analytics division. On October 2015, at the IBM Insight conference in Las Vegas, announced a redesigned version 11 and upgraded from Cognos BI software and renamed it Cognos Analytics. It allows you to create charts, maps, lists, or any other available report type using relational or multidimensional data sources. Cognos Event Studio: This tool allows you to assign a specific event that sends a notification to the stakeholder in your organization. Cognos Metric Studio: Cognos Metric Studio allows you to monitor and analyze business metrics of your organization by building a scorecard environment. It also helps you to establish criteria and then monitor your organization to see how it is responding as the changes made in the criteria.
